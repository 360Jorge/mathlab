## Problem
A number starts at $1$. You may repeatedly apply one move:
- replace $n$ by $n + 2$, or
- replace $n$ by $3n$.

Can you ever reach $2$?

---

## Hints

### Nudge
Start by trying small examples. Perform the operation a few times and write down what happens to the numbers.

### Focus
Look for a quantity that does **not change** when you apply the operation. Parity (even/odd) is often a good first thing to check.

### Key idea
Track the parity of the numbers involved in the operation. Does the move change whether they are even or odd?

### Finish
Once you identify a quantity whose parity never changes, compare the initial situation with the target situation. If their parity differs, the transformation is impossible.

---

## Solution
Both moves preserve oddness: $n \mapsto n+2$ keeps parity, and $n \mapsto 3n$ keeps parity.
Starting from $1$ (odd), you can only reach odd numbers, so you can never reach $2$ (even).
