## Problem
A number starts at $1$. You may repeatedly apply one move:
- replace $n$ by $n + 2$, or
- replace $n$ by $3n$.

Can you ever reach $2$?

## Hints
### Hint 1
What stays the same mod $2$ under each move?

### Hint 2
Check each move modulo $2$ explicitly.

## Solution
Both moves preserve oddness: $n \mapsto n+2$ keeps parity, and $n \mapsto 3n$ keeps parity.
Starting from $1$ (odd), you can only reach odd numbers, so you can never reach $2$ (even).
