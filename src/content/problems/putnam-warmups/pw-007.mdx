## Problem

Every integer greater than 1 can be written as a product of primes.

---

## Hints

### Nudge
This is a classic place to use contradiction. Suppose the statement fails somewhere.

### Focus
If there is a counterexample, choose the smallest one. Smallest-counterexample arguments often force the smaller pieces to behave well.

### Key idea
If your smallest bad integer $n$ is not prime, then it factors as $n=ab$ with smaller positive integers $a,b<n$.

### Finish
Why must both $a$ and $b$ already factor into primes? And what does that imply about $n$?

---

## Solution

Assume a smallest counterexample $n>1$ exists.

Then $n$ is not prime, so $n=ab$ with $1<a,b<n$.

By minimality, both $a$ and $b$ factor into primes.

Thus $n$ factors into primes — contradiction.

Therefore every integer greater than 1 factors into primes.
