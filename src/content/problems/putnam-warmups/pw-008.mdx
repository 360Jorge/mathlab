## Problem

Show that among any 6 integers, two have difference divisible by 5.

---

## Hints

### Nudge
Whenever a problem says “difference divisible by 5,” try thinking in terms of remainders modulo $5$.

### Focus
How many possible remainders can an integer have modulo $5$?

### Key idea
You are choosing more integers than there are remainder classes. That means at least two of them must land in the same class.

### Finish
If two integers have the same remainder modulo $5$, what does that tell you about their difference?

---

## Solution

There are only 5 residues mod 5.

With 6 integers, two share the same residue.

Thus their difference is divisible by 5.
