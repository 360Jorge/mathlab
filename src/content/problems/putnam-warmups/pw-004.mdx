## Problem

You start with the numbers $1, 1, 1, 1$ written on a board.

In one move, you may erase any two numbers $a, b$ and replace them with $a+b+1$.

Can you eventually obtain the number $2025$?

---

## Hints

### Nudge
Don’t try to jump straight to $2025$. First play with the process for a few moves and record what happens to the total sum.

### Focus
Each move replaces two numbers $a,b$ by a single number $a+b+1$. Compare the new total sum to the old one.

### Key idea
You are not really tracking the individual numbers. You are tracking a quantity that is easier to control: the sum of all numbers on the board, especially its parity.

### Finish
How many moves are required to go from 4 numbers down to 1 number? If the parity flips each move, what parity must the final number have?

---

## Solution

The total sum initially is $4$ (even).

Each move replaces $a+b$ with $a+b+1$, so the total sum increases by exactly $1$.

Thus parity flips every move.

After 3 moves (to reach one number), parity becomes odd.

But $2025$ is odd — so actually reachable.

Therefore **yes, it is possible**.
