## Problem

Start with two integers $a$ and $b$.

You may repeatedly replace the larger number by the difference of the two numbers.

Can the process continue forever?

---

## Hints

### Nudge
Try the process on a few concrete pairs like $(10,6)$, $(15,9)$, or $(7,2)$. What seems to be happening?

### Focus
Instead of tracking the numbers separately, look for a quantity that always gets smaller.

### Key idea
If $a>b$, the move replaces $(a,b)$ by $(a-b,b)$. Compare the new sum to the old sum.

### Finish
You now have a positive integer quantity that strictly decreases every step. Why does that rule out an infinite process?

---

## Solution

Each move replaces $(a,b)$ with $(a-b,b)$ where $a>b$.

The new sum is $(a-b)+b = a < a+b$.

Thus the sum strictly decreases and is always positive integer.

Therefore the process must terminate.

So **it cannot continue forever**.
