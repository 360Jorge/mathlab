## Problem

Prove that $2^n + 1$ is divisible by $3$ infinitely often.

---

## Hints

### Nudge
Compute the first few values of $2^n+1$ and reduce them modulo $3$.

### Focus
You should notice that the powers of $2$ modulo $3$ repeat in a very short cycle.

### Key idea
Find exactly when
$$
2^n \equiv -1 \pmod{3}.
$$
That is the same as asking when $2^n+1$ is divisible by $3$.

### Finish
Once you identify one infinite family of exponents $n$ for which this happens, you are done.

---

## Solution

Compute powers mod 3:

$$
2^1=2,\quad
2^2=1,\quad
2^3=2,\quad
2^4=1,\dots
$$

Cycle length is 2.

So $2^n \equiv 2 \pmod{3}$ for odd $n$.

Thus $2^n+1\equiv0\pmod{3}$ whenever $n$ is odd.

Hence infinitely many such $n$ exist.
